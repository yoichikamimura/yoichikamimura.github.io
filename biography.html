Yoichi Kamimura

>CV(En) PDF Download  >CV(Jp) PDF Download

1982 Born in Chiba, Japan
2008 Graduated from Department of Oil Painting,
  Tokyo University of the Arts
2010 Completed MA of Fine Arts, Tokyo University of the Arts
   
  Based in Helsinki, Finland


Drawing chiefly upon his field recordings, Kamimura experiments with methods that draw upon sight, hearing, and other senses to perceive different scenes. His extensive body of work includes sound installations, paintings, video works, sound performances, and audio works - unveiled in venues both within Japan and abroad.

Kamimura refers to the field-recording process as an act of "meditative hunting" - in the process, he acts an observer to the amorphous relationship between humankind and nature. Kamimura composes his sound installations by creating highly-immersive “sound-scapes,” many of which draw upon our own biology to create unique sensory experiences.

Recent major exhibitions and projects include "Waterforest" (hakari contemporary, Kyoto), "Awasi Art Week by LABVERDE2 (Awasi Iguazú, Iguazú, 2024), "LABVERDE Speculative Ecologies Residency 2023" (Manaus, 2023), "The View of Hanui"(Asia Culture Center, Gwangju, South Korea, 2023), "SIM Residency"(SIM Residency, Reykjavik, Iceland, 2022), "From Seeing to Acting" (Looiersgracht 60, Amsterdam, 2021), and "Michikusa: Walks with the Unknown" (Contemporary Art Center, Art Tower Mito, Mito, 2020).

As for music releases, Kamimura released the collaborative album Therme Vals (Vertical Music, Berlin, 2023) with France Jobin. In July 2025, he is set to release ryūhyō, a field recording album composed entirely of environmental sounds of drift ice in Shiretoko collected over a six-year period, through the Berlin-based label forms of minutiae. In September of the same year, he released Kōri no ryokō / Jäämatkailu, a collaborative ambient music album with Finnish artist Olli Aarni, through the Slovakian label Mappa.

Kamimura was awarded "the Sound of the Year Awards" in London for two consecutive years in 2023 and 2024. In the same year, he received the Special Jury Prize at "Phonurgia Nova 2023" in Paris.
